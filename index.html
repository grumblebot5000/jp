Joanna Petrone

Joanna Petrone is a freelance writer whose essays, profiles, and reported features have appeared in Slate, Popula, Vice, Avidly, Longreads, Good Company, the SF Chronicle, The Billfold, and The Awl. Joanna is an adoptive mother as well a gestational parent. She lives with her two elementary school age kids, husband, and extremely fluffy cat in Berkeley, CA where they enjoy hitting the local bookshops and candy shops and being active outdoors. She likes to get out of the house.

Joanna has an English degree from Stanford and an Education degree from UC-Berkeley and has taught middle school English and Social Studies in the Bay Area for over a decade. She is currently taking a break from the classroom as she works on a novel about female ambition, fertility, and archaeology set amongst the remains of a Neolithic settlement in Anatolia.
